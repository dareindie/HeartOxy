Train in Your Target Heart Rate Zone
Heart rate zone training is widely used by athletes and fitness enthusiasts to maintain optimal workout intensity.
Heart rate zones are calculated as percentages of a person's maximum heart rate.

Zone	% Max Heart Rate	Training Benefit
Warm-up	50–60%	Recovery and preparation
Fat Burn	60–70%	Endurance and fat metabolism
Aerobic	70–80%	Cardiovascular conditioning
Anaerobic	80–90%	High-intensity training
Peak	90–100%	Maximum performance

HeartOxy tracks these zones automatically using Apple Watch heart-rate data and provides live feedback to help users remain within their selected training zone.
This allows workouts to stay focused on the intended training intensity.

Apple Watch Heart Rate Accuracy
Apple Watch uses optical sensors to estimate heart rate by detecting changes in blood flow at the wrist using photoplethysmography (PPG).
Multiple scientific studies comparing wearable devices with electrocardiogram (ECG) measurements have demonstrated that Apple Watch heart-rate sensors provide highly accurate readings for fitness monitoring and exercise tracking.

Research evaluating wearable heart-rate monitors has reported strong agreement between Apple Watch heart-rate readings and clinical reference measurements, with correlation values reported around 0.96 when compared with ECG measurements.¹

Additional studies examining exercise conditions have found excellent agreement during walking, running, and moderate-to-high intensity exercise, demonstrating that wrist-based measurements from Apple Watch can provide reliable heart-rate data during workouts.²³

These findings indicate that Apple Watch provides high-quality heart rate measurements suitable for heart-rate zone training, workout monitoring, and cardiovascular fitness tracking.

When paired with intelligent training software like HeartOxy, users can gain training insights traditionally associated with specialized monitoring systems — without requiring additional wearable equipment.
